Case Study

A User Experience for UCF: Student Access Resource Center

A conceptualization into material view

By FRANCIS CACCAVALE

This design is a user experience to help UCF SARC (Student Access Resource Center) increase undergraduate pass rates, holistically.

A university needs a modern and holistic approach to increase undergraduate passing rates, especially for introductory courses given to first year students. As a resolve via sole concept and design, each student will create a UX design style ‘sticker-sheet’ which would instead feature personal attributes; whether that be a color palette, interests in their favorite typeface, a graph of their recent Spotify playlist and/or photographs instead of logos. This aggregate of stickersheets would then compile into an internal social network to create an economy of empathy and ultimately help passing rates.

Understanding the user as to divide and conquer

Twenty students were given a quesionaire via Google Forms with some example questions being:

  • What is your gender and class (Fr, So, Jr, Sr)?
  • Which social-network resonates with you most?
  • Are your social-network profiles public or private?
  • Have you had to delete any social-networking app from your phone at some point in time?

A competitive audit was conducted in order to identify limitations of other similar concepts already in production, only to create an upper bound for this design.
