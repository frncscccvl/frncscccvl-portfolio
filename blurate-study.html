An Interface Between Critics and Curators

Two user sets seek clean line of communication

By FRANCIS CACCAVALE

Critics will receive content for their site while curators will receive press for their exhibit, that is, a win-win scenario.

This interface will set a clean line of communication between two user sets, streamlining creation of content as to a blog or article. With this line of communication, critics can prepare their research and an ultimate goal of asking a curator for a private walkthrough personally or utilizing Google virtually.

Empathize

Museums often act iteratively with 'pop-up' like exhibitions focusing different themes, modernized buildings with new layouts, new artists, new archaelogical findings et cetera. Prospective museums receive a ton of grants and donations whether through government or private sources, hence making this industry quite observable and implicates value. Through secondary research of different news articles about upcoming exhibitions, what is missing are insights into the curator's perspective into the exhibition.
